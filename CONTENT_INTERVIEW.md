# Content & Brand Interview Guide — Insidin

## Context for the interviewer

You are helping produce content for a personal company website — **insidin.com** — for Jan Uyttenhove, a data architect and technology strategist based in Belgium. The site is the primary professional landing page for his consultancy, Insidin. It links to two related properties he owns:

- **King Context** (kingcontext.com) — a technical blog on data architecture and platform engineering, aimed at architects and engineers
- **DG2DMM** — a data management maturity framework he created, with its own placeholder site

The primary audience for insidin.com is **senior business and technology leaders** (CDOs, CTOs, VPs of Engineering/Data) who arrive via LinkedIn. The site needs to communicate credibility, expertise, and a clear value proposition — not a product catalogue.

The goal of this interview is to gather everything needed to:
1. Write or refine the copy for each section of the site
2. Define the brand voice with authenticity
3. Understand Jan's positioning relative to the market

**Format:** Conversational. Take notes. Push for concrete examples and specific language — avoid generic consultant-speak. Where Jan uses a phrase naturally, capture it verbatim; it's often better than anything written from scratch.

---

## Part 1 — Positioning & Value Proposition

These questions define what goes in the hero and the core "what we do" sections.

1. In one or two sentences, what does Insidin actually do? Don't describe the company — describe what changes for a client after working with you.

2. Why "Insidin"? What does the name mean to you and what should it signal to someone landing on the site?

3. Who is your ideal client? Be specific — what industry, what size, what role, what problem are they sitting with when they find you?

4. What problem do you solve that traditional large consultancies (McKinsey, Deloitte, Accenture) don't solve well? Where do they fall short?

5. What problem do you solve that a typical senior freelance contractor doesn't solve?

6. How would a past client describe what working with you was like — in their words, not yours?

7. Is there a type of engagement or client you actively avoid? What's a bad fit?

---

## Part 2 — Background & Credibility

These feed the About section and the personal brand narrative.

8. Walk me through the career arc that led to founding Insidin. What are the 2–3 defining experiences or decisions?

9. What was the moment — or the problem you kept seeing — that made you decide to go independent?

10. What have you built or delivered that you're most proud of? (Not necessarily the most impressive on paper — the one that felt most meaningful.)

11. You have 20+ years in data architecture. What has fundamentally changed in that time, and what has stayed stubbornly the same?

12. What's your actual relationship with AI right now — tool you use, thing you advise on, thing you're skeptical of, all of the above?

13. Are there public references, case studies, or clients you can name on the site? If not, what can you say — sectors, scale, outcomes — without naming names?

---

## Part 3 — Services

For each service, the goal is a crisp description plus a sense of how engagements typically look and what success looks like.

### Data & AI Architecture

14. What does a typical engagement look like — where does it start, how long does it run, what does it produce?

15. What's the thing clients think they need when they come to you, versus what they actually need?

16. What's a concrete outcome you can describe (anonymised if needed) — the before and after?

17. "Streaming" has been central to your work for a long time. Is that still the primary lens, or has the framing shifted toward AI/ML platforms, lakehouses, something else?

### DG2DMM Framework

18. What is DG2DMM in plain language — for someone who doesn't know the acronym?

19. What problem were you solving when you created it? What existing frameworks were failing organisations, and how?

20. Who uses it — is it a tool for consultants, for internal data leaders, for both?

21. Is it freely available, productised, licensed? What's the commercial model?

22. What does the DG2DMM site need to do — inform, generate leads, host documentation, something else?

### Venture Advisory

23. What does this actually involve — board seat, mentoring, co-investment, something else?

24. What kinds of startups do you work with? Stage, sector, founding team profile?

25. Is this an active part of the business, or more of a "by introduction only" sideline?

---

## Part 4 — King Context & Content Strategy

The company site will surface exec summaries of King Context posts. These questions shape how the two properties relate.

26. Who is the King Context reader, and how is that person different from the insidin.com visitor?

27. Which existing King Context posts best represent the thinking you want insidin.com to reflect? Pick 3–5.

28. What topics are you planning to write about in the next 6 months?

29. How often do you realistically publish? Daily, weekly, monthly?

30. You mentioned LinkedIn as the primary distribution channel. What do you typically post — articles, short takes, links to posts? What gets the most engagement?

31. Should insidin.com have its own blog/writing section with original content, or purely surface King Context? Or should some content appear on both?

---

## Part 5 — Voice & Tone

These questions help calibrate how the copy should sound.

32. Name 2–3 people (writers, thinkers, practitioners — anyone) whose communication style you respect and find credible. What is it about how they write or speak that works for you?

33. Name something in the B2B consulting world — a site, a LinkedIn voice, a firm's positioning — that you find hollow, performative, or irritating. What's wrong with it?

34. How formal should the site be? If you imagine a dinner with a potential client, are you in a suit or not?

35. Do you refer to yourself as "I" (personal brand) or "we" (company) on the site? Is Insidin a solo practice or does it have or aspire to have more people?

36. Is there a phrase, tagline, or sentence you've used — in a proposal, a talk, a conversation — that landed well and you've used again? Capture it here.

---

## Part 6 — Design & Visual Identity

These questions help validate or adjust the current design direction (navy / teal / amber palette, Plus Jakarta Sans / Inter typography).

37. When you look at the current demo: what feels right, what feels off, what's missing?

38. Are there websites — of any kind, not just consultancies — that you find visually impressive or credible? What do they have in common?

39. Do you have a professional photo you're happy to use? If not, is that something to commission?

40. Any hard constraints — colours associated with competitors, visual styles that feel wrong for the audience?

---

## Part 7 — Logistics & Priorities

41. What is the most important thing the site needs to do in the next 90 days?

42. Is there a launch milestone or deadline (conference, announcement, campaign) to work toward?

43. Who, if anyone, needs to approve content before it goes live?

44. Are the other two domains (King Context, DG2DMM) in scope for this round of work, or is insidin.com the priority?

---

## Deliverables expected from this interview

After the session, the interviewer should be able to produce:

- [ ] A revised hero headline and sub-headline (2–3 options)
- [ ] An About section (150–200 words, first person)
- [ ] Service descriptions for each of the 3 service cards (60–80 words each)
- [ ] 3 exec-summary blog cards with titles, summaries, and tags (from King Context)
- [ ] Contact section copy (2–3 sentences)
- [ ] A voice & tone reference note (half a page) for future content consistency
- [ ] A list of open questions or gaps to resolve before launch
