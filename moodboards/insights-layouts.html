AI strategy doesn't expire because organizations are slow. It expires because the landscape invalidates assumptions faster than planning cycles can absorb them.

The real question isn't whether you have a strategy. It's whether your strategy mechanism can keep up with a pace of change that treats static plans as liabilities. Most enterprises are still writing documents when they should be building adaptive structures.

Mar 15

The biggest risk in enterprise AI isn't the technology. It's distributed adoption without shared ownership.

One team experiments with copilots, another automates workflows, a third builds customer-facing features. Security worries, legal worries, data teams worry. But nobody owns the whole picture. The enterprise is already in motion, just without a shared management structure.

That's why capability mapping matters. Not to slow things down, but to make cross-functional responsibilities visible before the gap between distributed use and coordinated control becomes too large.

Mar 9

Governance that relies on people remembering to follow a process will always decay. Governance embedded in the platform compounds.

That's the core insight behind computational data governance. Stop treating governance as a separate organisational layer and start treating it as platform capability.
