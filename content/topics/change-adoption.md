The technology is rarely the hard part. The hard part is everything that happens around it: ownership models, decision-making patterns, the gap between what people agree to in a steering committee and what they actually do on Monday morning.

Most architecture and strategy engagements treat change management as an afterthought, a workstream that runs in parallel but never truly integrates. Adoption-first thinking inverts this: it starts with what it takes for change to land and works backward to the technology decisions.

This topic collects practical thinking on adoption, organisational effectiveness, and the patterns that make or break transformation programs.
