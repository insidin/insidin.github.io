AI risk discourse oscillates between existential speculation and dismissive optimism. Neither serves organisations trying to make practical decisions about AI adoption. What's needed is a grounded, evidence-based assessment of the risks that actually matter at the enterprise level: bias, reliability, security, regulatory exposure, and the organisational risks of deploying systems that nobody fully understands.

This topic collects critical thinking on AI risk, with a focus on practical risk assessment frameworks and honest evaluation of what organisations should and shouldn't be worried about.
